The McKee Family

Deborah Jane McKee, wife of Robert Hays, and father Thomas McKee, husband of Margaret Blaine

Andrew Mackey, or McKee, as the family was later called, was born in Londonderry and immigrated to America near Chester, PA before the Revolution, most likely with his parents. He served in several regiments during the war and there is a record of his enlistment in the Cumberland Valley in the summer of 1781 which includes the following physical description: "5'8" tall, black hair, grey eyes, dark complexion, born in Ireland, a weaver."

His son Thomas, pictured above, was born in Cumberland, after which the family moved to Western PA, finally settling in Armstrong Co. Deborah Jane McKee Hays' son Thomas Hays, served in the Civil War, and her daughter-in-law Keziah Foster Hays also played a role in the Civil War, when she and some classmates made a flag that was carried throughout the Civil War by some local boys, and kept with them, concealed, even while in Confederate prison. This flag was presented by Keziah & Deborah Jane's son, Thomas Hays to the Soldiers & Sailors Memorial Hall in Pittsburgh, PA in 1912.

Please note: any McKee-Mackay ancestry beyond this point is based on limited research so far, family trees of others and is NOT proven. Use it as a starting point. Andrew was in any case in America by 1766 when he is named in land warrants along with William & John Mckee. He named his first male children William & John. I also have a will for John McKie from about 1712 in Scotland, naming a son John. Based on this, I now consider it more likely that in contrast to what is below, Andrew, William & John were the sons of the John McKie mentioned below as eldest son of John McKie & Esther Hawthorne, and not of Andrew, a younger brother. The elder John is likely the one who died in 1712 and his son John died in 1765, which could have been the point at which Andrew, William & John immigrated. It is of course possible that Andrew was the son of the younger brother Andrew, but we would then have expected him to name his 1st or 2nd son Andrew, not his 5th son .
